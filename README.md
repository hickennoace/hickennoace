### 🧭 Who I am

I'm an **Economics & Management** undergrad at **The Open University**, and I'm the person who can't look at a spreadsheet without asking *"okay, but what is this actually telling us?"*

That instinct is where finance and data analysis meet for me. Every project here starts the same way — a **real dataset**, a **question worth money or meaning**, and a disciplined hunt for the signal underneath the noise. I care less about flashy charts and more about conclusions that survive scrutiny.

```text
finance theory  ──►  real-world data  ──►  rigorous analysis  ──►  a decision someone can act on
```

### 🔬 How I work a dataset

<table>
<tr><td>

```text
1 · Frame   →  What decision does this answer change?
2 · Clean   →  Trust the data before trusting the chart.
3 · Explore →  Distributions, outliers, relationships.
4 · Model   →  Correlation, regression, time-series — stated assumptions.
5 · Stress  →  Does it hold out-of-sample / across segments?
6 · Tell    →  One clear conclusion a non-analyst can use.
```

</td></tr>
</table>

<details>
<summary><b>📈 Ethereum Macro Analysis — deeper look</b></summary>

<br>

A quantitative study of **ETH-USD over 2021–2026** against three benchmarks — **BTC, the NASDAQ-100, and the US dollar (DXY)**. It computes risk metrics, builds a correlation matrix across assets, and runs **OLS regression** to test how much of Ethereum's movement the macro picture explains. The takeaway isn't "number go up" — it's *what ETH is correlated to, and when that breaks down.*

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>🛒 Customer Behaviour — deeper look</b></summary>

<br>

A full revenue post-mortem for an online store (a Minecraft network): segmenting players, tracing **where the money actually comes from**, and quantifying the **leak — cart abandonment**. Data is wrangled in **Python**, modelled in **SQLite/SQL**, and surfaced in a **Power BI** dashboard, ending in concrete "what to do about it" recommendations.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>🚔 L.A. Crime Rate — deeper look</b></summary>

<br>

A **7-page Power BI report** built from roughly **853,000** LAPD incidents (2020–late 2023). It covers an executive overview, geographic & operational patterns, an hour × weekday heatmap, victim demographics, weapon and crime-type breakdowns, and case-status / investigation analysis — driven by a clean star-schema model and a library of **DAX measures** (YoY, rolling averages, reporting-lag, rankings).

</details>
